Universal Gravitation

Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation is an important in-depth topic in AP Physics C. You need to master the derivation of gravitational potential energy and orbital mechanics.

Law of Universal Gravitation

F=Gr2m1​m2​​

In vector form: F12​=−Gr2m1​m2​​r^12​

Where:

  • G = gravitational constant (6.67×10−11 N⋅m2/kg2)
  • m1​,m2​ = masses of two objects
  • r = distance between centers of mass

Gravitational Potential Energy

Gravitational potential energy (with zero potential energy at infinity):

U=−rGMm​

Derivation: U=−∫∞r​r′2GMm​dr′=−rGMm​

Gravitational Field

Gravitational field strength (force per unit mass):

g​=−r2GM​r^

Gravitational Force Inside a Uniform Sphere

For a uniform sphere of radius R and density ρ, the gravitational force on a mass m at distance r from the center (where r<R) is:

F=−34πGρm​⋅r

This is a linear restoring force directed toward the center, similar to a spring force. An object dropped through a tunnel in such a planet would execute simple harmonic motion.

See: Example 7 - Ball in Tunnel Through Planet

Escape Speed

The minimum speed required to escape from a planet’s surface:

vescape​=R2GM​​

Example Problem

Example: Earth has mass M=5.97×1024 kg and radius R=6.37×106 m. Find the escape speed from Earth’s surface.

Solution: vescape​=6.37×1062×6.67×10−11×5.97×1024​​ =1.25×108​≈1.12×104 m/s=11.2 km/s

The inverse-square law structure of gravitation appears in both electrostatics and magnetism:

Coulomb’s Law (Electrostatics)

F=ke​r2q1​q2​​

Identical mathematical form to gravitation, but:

  • Charge can be positive or negative (attractive or repulsive)
  • Electric force is much stronger (ke​≫G)

See Electric Charge and Coulomb’s Law

Gauss’s Law

Gravitation has a Gauss-like law for the gravitational flux: ∮g​⋅dA=−4πGMenc​

This is directly analogous to Gauss’s Law for electric fields.

Biot-Savart Law (Magnetism)

The magnetic field from a current element: dB=4πμ0​​r2Idl×r^​

Also follows inverse-square dependence (for the field magnitude).
